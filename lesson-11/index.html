Recent Heavy Snowfall In Southeast Idaho

Recent heavy snowstorm dropped 24 to 36 inches in the Southeast region of Idaho during a two day period this week. The snowstorm caused multiple problems throughout the region. Several highways were closed to prevent accidents and stranded motorists. Several small towns and rural neighborhoods reported electrical outages. All electrical outages were resolved and power restored to the areas. Many schools canceled classes on Thursday and Friday due to power outages.

The heavy snowstorm brought much excitement to snow enthusiasts in the region. Several ski resorts in the area have reported that the storm dropped enough snow to allow an early opening day. With the recent snowfall and snow making, many resorts report that their snow base is between 60 to 84 inches deep. All runs accessible to groomers have been groomed and there is 36 inches of new powder. Ski resorts state that ski lift tickets are available on their websites or starting at Monday morning on opening day.
